VO2 Max

The single most powerful predictor of how long you'll live. How to test it, train it, and what the data actually says.

VO2 max — maximum oxygen uptake — measures your body's ceiling for aerobic energy production. It's expressed as mL of oxygen per kg of body weight per minute (mL/kg/min). In 2018, a landmark study of 122,007 adults (Mandsager et al., JAMA Network Open) established VO2 max as the single most powerful predictor of all-cause mortality — stronger than smoking, diabetes, or coronary artery disease. The difference between the bottom 25% and top 2.5% of fitness was a 5x mortality gap. Peter Attia calls it "the most powerful marker we have." The good news: VO2 max is trainable at any age, and even small improvements yield dramatic mortality reductions.

The evidence
Why VO2 Max Predicts Longevity
122,007 adults. 5x mortality gap. Stronger than smoking, diabetes, or heart disease as a predictor.
Mandsager et al. (2018, JAMA Network Open) — 122,007 adults, median 8.4yr follow-up. Cardiorespiratory fitness was the most powerful predictor of survival. Low fitness carried higher mortality risk than smoking (41%), diabetes (40%), or coronary artery disease (29%). The 5x gap between lowest and highest fitness groups was the largest effect size observed.
Kokkinos et al. (2022, JACC) — 750,000+ US veterans. Each 1-MET increase (~3.5 mL/kg/min VO2 max) was associated with a 13–15% reduction in mortality risk. Consistent across age, sex, BMI, and race. No group was "too old" or "too unfit" to benefit.
The biggest jump is from low to below-average — moving from the bottom 25% to even below-average fitness reduces mortality by ~50%. This is the single most impactful improvement. You don't need to be elite — you need to not be sedentary.
No upper limit to benefit — even going from "high" to "elite" fitness still reduced mortality. There is no point at which additional fitness stops paying dividends. The dose-response curve never flattens.
Each 1 mL/kg/min increase = 45 more days of life — a JACC study with 46 years of follow-up found each unit increase in VO2 max at midlife was associated with 45 additional days of longevity. Small improvements compound into years.
🧬
The biology
What VO2 Max Actually Measures
Heart, lungs, blood, mitochondria — your body's entire oxygen delivery and utilization chain.
VO2 max reflects your entire oxygen chain — cardiac output (how much blood your heart pumps), hemoglobin efficiency (oxygen-carrying capacity), peripheral extraction (how well muscles extract O2 from blood), and mitochondrial density (how effectively cells convert O2 to ATP). It's a whole-system readout.
Declines ~10% per decade after 30 — without training, VO2 max drops steadily with age. By 70, an untrained person may have half the aerobic capacity they had at 30. This decline directly maps to functional independence loss.
The independence threshold: ~18 mL/kg/min — below this level, basic activities of daily living (climbing stairs, carrying groceries, getting off the floor) become difficult or impossible. Attia frames training as "banking" VO2 max now to stay above this line at 80+.
Attia's "decade younger" rule — aim for VO2 max in the "Excellent" tier for someone 10 years younger than you. If you're 50, target the Excellent range for a 40-year-old. This builds the buffer you need to stay independent through your 80s and 90s.

🎯 The Five Training Zones

The 80/20 rule: spend 80% of training in Zone 2 and 20% in Zone 4–5. This polarized approach produces the best VO2 max improvements in both recreational and elite athletes.

Training protocols

How to Train VO2 Max

🚶
Foundation
Zone 2 Base (80% of Training)
150–180 min/week. Nasal breathing. Conversational pace. Build the aerobic engine.
150–180 min/week minimum — 3–5 sessions of 30–60 min at Zone 2 (60–70% HR max). This is brisk walking, easy jogging, cycling, swimming, or rowing where you can hold a full conversation. If you can't talk, you're too fast.
Zone 2 builds mitochondrial density — this is where your body creates new mitochondria (mitochondrial biogenesis) and improves fat oxidation. More mitochondria = more efficient energy production = higher VO2 max ceiling.
Nasal breathing as your governor — if you can breathe exclusively through your nose, you're in Zone 2. The moment you need to open your mouth, you've crossed into Zone 3. This is the simplest intensity test.
Zone 2 is the most underrated longevity tool — it doesn't feel productive because it's not hard. That's the point. You're building metabolic infrastructure, not fitness through suffering. Consistency matters more than intensity here.
⚡
Performance
VO2 Max Intervals (20% of Training)
Norwegian 4x4. Hard efforts that push your ceiling. 1–2 sessions per week.
Norwegian 4x4 protocol — 4 intervals of 4 minutes at 90–95% HR max, with 3 minutes active recovery between intervals. Developed by Dr. Ulrik Wisløff at NTNU. The most well-studied VO2 max interval protocol in the literature. Total session: ~35 min including warm-up and cooldown.
1–2 sessions per week maximum — VO2 max intervals are high-stress. More than 2 per week risks overtraining, elevated cortisol, and injury. The adaptation happens during recovery, not during the session. Respect the dose.
Alternative: Tabata-style (for time-crunched) — 8 rounds of 20 sec all-out effort, 10 sec rest. Only 4 minutes of work. Effective for VO2 max but brutal — not for beginners. Use 1x/week as a supplement to the 4x4, not a replacement.
Any modality works — running, cycling, rowing, swimming, ski erg. Choose the modality where you can safely push to 90%+ HR max. Cycling and rowing are joint-friendly for heavier or older athletes.

🧪 How to Test Your VO2 Max

🏥Gold standard: metabolic lab test — treadmill or cycle ergometer with a mask measuring gas exchange. Costs $150–300. Available at sports performance labs and some gyms. Most accurate by far.
⌚Wearable estimate (Garmin, Apple Watch, WHOOP) — uses HR data and activity patterns to estimate. Accuracy: ±5–10%. Good for tracking trends over time, but absolute numbers can be off. Better than nothing.
🏃Cooper 12-minute run test — run as far as possible in 12 minutes. Use the formula: VO2 max = (distance in meters - 504.9) / 44.73. Free, simple, reasonably accurate for most fitness levels.
📅Test annually, track monthly via wearable — lab test once a year to calibrate. Use wearable estimates monthly to track trajectory. The trend matters more than any single number.

🔗 VO2 Max Stacking

🏋️
+ Strength training
Muscle mass supports VO2 max by increasing mitochondrial sites and improving oxygen extraction. Do both.
💚
+ HRV tracking
Use HRV to guide training intensity. Low HRV = Zone 2 day. High HRV = green light for intervals.
🫁
+ Breathwork
CO2 tolerance training improves oxygen delivery efficiency. Nasal breathing during Zone 2 trains this automatically.
🧊
+ Cold plunge
Post-interval cold exposure reduces inflammation. On off-days only — avoid cold within 4 hrs of hard training.
💊
+ Creatine
5g/day. Supports ATP regeneration during intervals and may improve power output at VO2 max intensity.
