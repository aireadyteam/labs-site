Healthy Diet

The longevity plate, protein optimization, the glucose order trick, and a complete food-first framework for healthspan.

Nutrition science is noisy, but the signal is clear: eat mostly whole, minimally processed foods, prioritize protein, maximize plant diversity, and use quality fats. The longest-lived populations on earth — Blue Zones, Mediterranean cohorts, Okinawan centenarians — all converge on a pattern: abundant vegetables, adequate protein, quality fats, minimal processed food, and no single macronutrient dogma. This cheat sheet distills the consensus from Attia, Patrick, Lyon, and Inchauspé into an actionable framework you can implement at your next meal. No ideology, no elimination diets — just the evidence.

Principle 01
Protein Is the Master Macro
0.7–1g per lb body weight. 30g+ per meal. The foundation of body composition and longevity.
Target: 0.7–1g protein per lb body weight — this range is supported by the International Society of Sports Nutrition, Examine.com analysis, and longevity physicians like Peter Attia and Gabrielle Lyon. The RDA of 0.36g/lb is the minimum to avoid deficiency, not the optimum for healthspan.
30g+ per meal for muscle protein synthesis (MPS) — the leucine threshold for triggering MPS is ~2.5g, which typically requires 30–40g of high-quality protein per sitting. Spreading protein across 3–4 meals optimizes anabolic response, especially over age 40.
Muscle mass is the #2 longevity predictor — after VO2 max, skeletal muscle mass is the strongest predictor of healthspan outcomes. You cannot build or maintain muscle without adequate protein. Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) begins in your 30s.
Protein kills cravings — protein is the most satiating macronutrient. High-protein meals reduce ghrelin (hunger hormone) and increase PYY (satiety signal). If you're struggling with snacking or overeating, protein deficiency is often the root cause.
📊
Principle 02
The Glucose Order Trick
Eat fiber first → protein and fat → carbs last. Reduces glucose spikes up to 73%.
Order: fiber → protein/fat → carbs — Jessie Inchauspé (Glucose Goddess) popularized this research: eating vegetables and fiber first creates a physical barrier in your intestine that slows glucose absorption from carbs eaten afterward. Same meal, same macros — just different order.
Reduces glucose spikes by up to 73% — a 2015 study in Diabetes Care (Shukla et al.) confirmed that eating vegetables and protein before carbohydrates significantly reduced post-meal glucose and insulin excursions compared to eating carbs first.
Vinegar hack — 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar in water 10–20 min before a carb-heavy meal reduces the glucose spike by 20–30%. The acetic acid slows gastric emptying and inhibits the enzyme that breaks starch into glucose.
Walk after eating — a 10–15 minute walk after meals clears glucose from the bloodstream via muscle contraction (GLUT4 translocation). This is one of the most underrated metabolic interventions — free, easy, and immediately effective.
🫒
Principle 03
Quality Fats — EVOO as Foundation
Extra virgin olive oil as primary fat. Omega-3s from fish. Avoid seed oils.
EVOO is the longevity fat — the Mediterranean diet's health benefits are largely attributable to high EVOO consumption. Oleocanthal (its polyphenol) has anti-inflammatory effects comparable to ibuprofen. Aim for 2–4 tbsp/day on salads, vegetables, and finished dishes.
Omega-3s: EPA + DHA from fatty fish — wild salmon, sardines, mackerel, anchovies 2–3x/week. If not eating fish regularly, supplement with 2–3g combined EPA/DHA. Target an Omega-3 Index of 8–12% (measurable via blood test).
Cooking fats: butter, ghee, coconut oil, avocado oil — these have high smoke points and stable fatty acid profiles under heat. Save EVOO for low-heat cooking, dressings, and finishing.
Minimize seed oils — soybean, canola, corn, sunflower, safflower. These are high in omega-6 linoleic acid, oxidize easily under heat, and are ubiquitous in processed food. Reducing seed oil intake is one of the simplest dietary upgrades.
🌱
Principle 04
30+ Plant Species Per Week
Gut microbiome diversity correlates directly with plant diversity in your diet.
The American Gut Project (11,000+ participants) found that people who eat 30+ different plant species per week have significantly more diverse gut microbiomes than those eating fewer than 10, regardless of whether they identify as vegan, vegetarian, or omnivore.
Every plant counts — herbs (basil, cilantro, parsley), spices (turmeric, cumin, ginger), seeds (chia, flax, pumpkin), nuts, grains, legumes, fruits, and vegetables all count as separate species. Most people hit 30 more easily than they think once they start counting.
Cruciferous vegetables are non-negotiable — broccoli, cauliflower, kale, Brussels sprouts, arugula. These contain sulforaphane, which activates the Nrf2 pathway (your body's master antioxidant switch) and supports glutathione production, estrogen metabolism, and detoxification.
Prebiotic fiber feeds your good bacteria — garlic, onions, leeks, asparagus, bananas, oats. These contain inulin and FOS that feed Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus — the strains associated with immune health, mood, and metabolic function.

🚫 Minimize or Eliminate

🍬Added sugar — drives insulin resistance, glycation, inflammation, liver fat. Target <25g/day. Read every label.
🫙Seed oils — soybean, canola, corn, sunflower. High omega-6, oxidize easily. Ubiquitous in restaurants and packaged food.
🏭Ultra-processed food — if it has a barcode and 15+ ingredients, it's engineered for overconsumption. Single-ingredient foods are the goal.
🍞Refined carbs — white bread, pasta, crackers. Hit your bloodstream as fast as sugar. Swap for whole grains or skip entirely.
🥤Liquid calories — soda, juice, sweetened coffee, smoothies from chains. These bypass satiety signals and spike glucose.
🧪Artificial sweeteners — emerging evidence suggests they disrupt gut microbiome composition and may maintain sugar cravings despite zero calories.

🔗 Diet Stacking — Synergies

⏰
+ Intermittent Fasting
Compress eating to 16:8 window. Protein targets still apply — just hit them in fewer meals.
🏋️
+ Training
Prioritize protein within 2 hrs post-workout. Carbs post-training when muscles are insulin-sensitized.
🦠
+ Gut Health
30+ plants feeds diversity. Fermented foods (kimchi, sauerkraut, yogurt) add live probiotics.
🛡️
+ Glutathione
Sulfur-rich foods (cruciferous, garlic, eggs) provide cysteine — the rate-limiting GSH precursor.
🩸
+ Sugar Fasting
Eliminate added sugar for 30 days to reset palate, restore insulin sensitivity, and break cravings.
