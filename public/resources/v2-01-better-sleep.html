Sleep isn't rest — it's active biological repair. During sleep, your brain clears metabolic waste via the glymphatic system at 10x waking efficiency, growth hormone pulses drive tissue repair, memories consolidate, and hormones rebalance. Cutting sleep by even one hour reduces insulin sensitivity by up to 30%, increases cortisol, and impairs cognition more than legal intoxication. A 2023 study of 34,000+ participants confirmed that nighttime temperature is one of the strongest environmental determinants of sleep quality. If your sleep is broken, nothing else you optimize will reach its full potential.

The six protocols

Each card contains the full evidence-based breakdown. Start with the one easiest to implement tonight.

🌡️
Protocol 01
Cool Your Core
Bedroom 60–67°F. Warm shower trick. Warm socks, cool core.
Bedroom: 60–67°F (15–19°C) — your core must drop ~2–3°F to initiate sleep. Cleveland Clinic and National Sleep Foundation converge on this range. A 2023 study of 34,000+ adults confirmed quality declines as ambient temp rises above 60°F.
Cooling mattress pad — Eight Sleep or ChiliPad for precise temperature control. Dynamic cooling (cooler at onset, warmer mid-sleep) mirrors your natural thermoregulation curve.
Warm shower 60–90 min before bed — a 2019 meta-analysis (Haghayegh et al., 5,322 participants) confirmed passive body heating reduces sleep onset latency by ~36% via the post-shower core temperature drop.
Warm extremities, cool core — warm socks dilate peripheral blood vessels, accelerating core heat dump. Warm feet = cooler core = faster sleep.
🔴
Protocol 02
Control Your Light
Blue light suppresses melatonin ~50%. Morning sun anchors your clock.
Blue light suppresses melatonin ~50% — a 2015 PNAS study (Chang et al.) showed screens before bed suppress melatonin, delay circadian rhythm, reduce REM, and impair next-morning alertness. Switch to amber/red lighting (1800–2200K) after sunset.
Orange/red lens glasses after dark — clear "computer glasses" are largely ineffective for melatonin protection. Use true orange or red lenses if screens are unavoidable.
10 min morning sunlight within 30 min of waking — outdoor light (~10,000 lux even overcast vs ~500 indoor) sets your SCN, triggers cortisol awakening response, and programs melatonin release 14–16 hrs later. The #1 circadian anchor.
Progressive dimming after sunset — full brightness at dinner, 50% by 8pm, near-darkness at bedtime. Your SCN reads intensity as a time signal.
⏰
Protocol 03
Anchor Your Rhythm
Same wake time daily. Avoid social jet lag. Align light, meals, exercise.
Fixed wake time every day — including weekends. Even 30 min of variation fragments sleep architecture. The human circadian pacemaker has a near-24-hour period (Czeisler, 1999).
Consistent bedtime ±30 min — your body anticipates sleep and begins melatonin + temperature decline on schedule. Irregular times confuse this cascade.
Avoid social jet lag — sleeping in 2+ hrs on weekends = biological jet lag that takes 3–4 days to recover from.
Triple anchor — light (primary), food timing (secondary), exercise (tertiary). Keep all three consistent to reinforce a unified rhythm.
☕
Protocol 04
Time Your Inputs
Caffeine by noon. Last meal 3hrs before bed. Alcohol kills REM.
Caffeine half-life: 5–6 hrs — a 2pm coffee means ~50% is active at 8pm. Cut by noon (2pm max). Includes tea, pre-workout, dark chocolate.
Last meal 3+ hrs before bed — late eating raises core temp and activates the sympathetic NS. Eating within 2 hrs of bed increases onset latency and reduces efficiency.
Alcohol decimates REM — suppresses REM by 20–40%, causes mid-cycle awakenings, fragments architecture. Even 1–2 drinks measurably impair quality.
Front-load hydration — heavy fluid after 6pm causes nocturia. A single bathroom trip fragments sleep cycles significantly.

💊 Evidence-Based Sleep Supplements

Add one at a time, one per week. Track sleep quality to isolate what works for you.

Magnesium GlycinateStrong — 2025 RCT
200–400mg elemental, before bed
2025 RCT (155 adults, Nature and Science of Sleep) confirmed significant insomnia improvement vs placebo. Glycinate form provides calming glycine + high absorption. ~50% of adults are deficient.
L-TheanineModerate — multiple RCTs
200mg, before bed
Amino acid from green tea. Promotes alpha brain wave activity — calm, non-sedating relaxation. No tolerance buildup. Safe for nightly use.
GlycineModerate — clinical trials
3g, before bed
Lowers core body temperature via peripheral vasodilation — a direct sleep-onset trigger. 2007 trial showed improved quality, reduced fatigue, better next-day cognition.
ApigeninEmerging — preclinical
50mg, before bed
Flavonoid from chamomile. Binds GABA-A receptors. Popularized by Huberman. As of 2026, zero human trials on isolated apigenin at supplement doses for sleep. Promising but not yet proven.

🛏️ The 60-Minute Wind-Down

After 2–3 weeks, your brain begins releasing melatonin automatically when this routine starts.

T–60 min
Screens off, lights dim
All devices down. Switch to amber/red lighting. The hardest step and the most impactful.
Non-negotiable
T–45 min
Warm shower or bath
Triggers the post-warming core temperature drop. Alternatively: gentle stretching or yoga nidra.
T–30 min
Supplements + calm activity
Take Mg, L-theanine, apigenin. Physical book, journal, light conversation. No screens, no news.
T–10 min
Breathwork: 4-7-8
Inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s. 4–8 cycles. Shifts ANS to parasympathetic. Alt: 10-min NSDR session.
T–0
Lights out
60–67°F, blackout dark, phone in another room, mouth tape if tolerated.
Target: asleep within 15 min
