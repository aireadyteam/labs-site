Sugar Fasting

A 30-day reset that restores insulin sensitivity, breaks cravings, and recalibrates your palate. The single most impactful dietary elimination.

Added sugar is the single most damaging ingredient in the modern diet. It drives insulin resistance, chronic inflammation, liver fat accumulation, glycation (accelerated aging), and dopaminergic reward hijacking. The average American consumes 77g of added sugar per day — over 3x the AHA recommendation of <25g. Animal studies suggest sugar activates reward pathways more powerfully than cocaine. A 30-day sugar fast resets your palate, restores insulin sensitivity, breaks the craving cycle, and reveals how much of your energy, mood, and body composition was being sabotaged by sugar. This is the highest-impact single dietary change you can make.

The 30-day protocol — eliminate ALL added sugar for 30 days. This includes obvious sources (candy, soda, desserts) AND hidden sources (sauces, dressings, bread, yogurt, granola). Read every label. Anything ending in -ose (sucrose, fructose, maltose) or listed as syrup, nectar, or juice concentrate counts.

What happens during the reset — days 1-5: cravings peak, irritability, headaches (sugar withdrawal is real). Days 5-14: cravings diminish, energy stabilizes. Days 14-30: palate recalibrates, natural foods taste sweeter, energy and mood improve measurably. The rewiring is neurological.

Whole fruit is fine — the fiber in whole fruit dramatically slows fructose absorption and provides micronutrients. Fruit juice is not fine — it's sugar water with the fiber removed. Eat the apple, don't drink the juice.

After 30 days — reintroduce selectively and notice how your body reacts. Most people find they no longer crave sugar the way they did. Some added sugar in social contexts is fine — the goal is breaking the daily dependency, not lifelong abstinence.
