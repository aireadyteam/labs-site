Metabolic Training

Strength meets conditioning. High-intensity resistance circuits that build muscle, burn fat, and improve metabolic health simultaneously.

EPOC

Metabolic training (MetCon) combines compound resistance exercises with minimal rest periods to create a dual-stimulus effect: you build muscle AND elevate your metabolic rate simultaneously. The key mechanism is EPOC (excess post-exercise oxygen consumption) — the elevated calorie burn that continues for 24-48 hours after an intense session. Unlike traditional cardio (which burns calories only during the session), metabolic training creates a metabolic debt your body spends hours repaying. This is the most time-efficient training modality for body composition change.

Compound movements only

Compound movements only — squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press, row, pull-up, lunge. These recruit the most muscle mass and create the largest metabolic demand. Isolation exercises (bicep curls, leg extensions) don't create enough systemic stress for meaningful EPOC.
⏱️
02
Rest periods are the variable
traditional strength training: 2-3 min rest. Metabolic training: 30-60 sec rest ...
Rest periods are the variable — traditional strength training: 2-3 min rest. Metabolic training: 30-60 sec rest or circuit format with no rest between exercises. The shortened rest keeps heart rate elevated (Zone 3-4) while the resistance stimulus preserves or builds muscle. This is what creates the EPOC effect.
📋
03
Sample circuit

Sample circuit — 4 rounds: Goblet squat x10, Push-ups x12, KB swing x15, Renegade row x8/side, Burpee x6. 30 sec rest between exercises, 90 sec between rounds. Total time: 25-30 min. Adjust weights to maintain form through all rounds.
🔄
04
Periodize intelligently

Periodize intelligently — don't do MetCon every day. 3-4 sessions/week with at least one rest day between sessions. Pair with dedicated Zone 2 cardio (2-3x/week) and one pure strength day for a complete program. Recovery is where adaptation happens.
